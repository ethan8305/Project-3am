## Start with the person

Future planning works best when it begins with the person being cared for, not
only with documents. Before thinking about wills or trusts, it can help to
picture what a good, flourishing life looks like for them: where they feel
calm, what they enjoy, and the routines that matter.

## What to think about

- What does a good day look like for them?
- What are their interests, talents and preferences?
- Where do they want more independence, and where do they want support?
- Who are the people they trust and feel close to?

## Involving the person being cared for

Wherever it is possible, include the person in these conversations, in a way
that suits how they communicate. This is their life. Their voice can shape the
plan, even where they need support to take part.

## A place to begin

You can use the blank Future Caregiver Folder to gather these thoughts in one
place, at your own pace. It is a private template: nothing is uploaded or
stored.
