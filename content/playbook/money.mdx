## Why this matters

Money planning is often the part that feels heaviest. Broken into pieces, it
becomes more manageable: first, setting something aside over time, and second,
making sure it reaches the person safely and is managed well.

## What to think about

- How might money be set aside steadily, rather than all at once?
- How would it be managed and reach the person after a caregiver is no longer
  around?
- Which tools are you exploring: a will, a trust, CPF nomination, insurance?
- Who would you trust to manage money carefully on the person's behalf?

## How this links to the legal tools

The resource hub explains the individual tools in plain language. Money
planning often brings several of them together:

- A **will** and **CPF nomination** say where things go.
- A **trust** can manage money over time.
- **Insurance** can be one source of funds.
- An **LPA** or **deputyship** can cover who makes decisions.

## A safety note

Do not write full bank account or policy numbers into any document you might
share. Keep those details private and secure.
