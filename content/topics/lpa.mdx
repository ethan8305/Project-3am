## What an LPA is

A Lasting Power of Attorney, often shortened to LPA, is a legal document. It
lets a person, while they are still able to decide things for themselves,
choose one or more trusted people to make decisions for them later, if a time
comes when they can no longer make those decisions on their own.

The person who makes the LPA is choosing in advance. The people they choose are
sometimes called donees or attorneys, depending on where you are.

## Why the timing matters

An LPA has to be made while the person still has the ability to understand the
decision and make it themselves. This is an important point: it is something
that is put in place ahead of time, not afterwards.

## When it tends to matter

People often look at an LPA when they want to:

- Choose for themselves who would step in, rather than leave it to a court.
- Make sure decisions about their care and money can be made smoothly if their
  ability to decide changes.

## Thinking about the person you care for

If the person you care for is able to understand and make this choice, an LPA
can be a way for them to stay in control of who supports them in future. It
centres their own wishes. Whether an LPA is possible depends on the person
being able to understand the decision at the time it is made.

If a person is not able to make this kind of decision, a different route, such
as deputyship, may come up instead. See the deputyship topic.
