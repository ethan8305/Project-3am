## What a CPF nomination is

A CPF nomination is a way of saying who should receive your Central Provident
Fund (CPF) savings after you die, and how much each person should receive. CPF
savings are not covered by a will, which is why a separate nomination exists.

If there is no nomination, your CPF savings are handled under a set process,
which can take time and may not reflect what you would have chosen.

## When it tends to matter

A CPF nomination tends to matter when you want to:

- Decide who receives your CPF savings, rather than leave it to a default
  process.
- Make sure savings reach the people you intend, without extra delay.

## Thinking about the person you care for

If you are planning to provide for the person you care for, it can help to look
at CPF nomination alongside your will, so the two work together rather than
pulling in different directions. How best to do that depends on your own
circumstances.
