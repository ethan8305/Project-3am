## What it is

The Special Needs Trust Company, often shortened to SNTC, is a non-profit trust
company in Singapore. It helps families set aside money in a trust for a person
with special needs, and it manages that money and arranges payouts according to
a care plan the family helps shape.

Because it is non-profit, it is designed to be more affordable than setting up
a private trust, which can make it an option for more families.

## When it tends to matter

People often look at an SNTC trust when they want to:

- Provide steady, managed financial support for a person with special needs
  after the caregivers are no longer able to provide it.
- Have a trusted organisation manage payouts, rather than relying on one
  individual.
- Set this up at a lower cost than a private trust.

## Thinking about the person being cared for

The aim is to support the life the person wants, not only to hold money. Where
it is possible, it can help to involve the person being cared for in shaping the
care plan, so that payouts reflect their own preferences and needs.

## How it links to other tools

An SNTC trust often sits alongside other tools. For example, CPF savings, a will
or insurance might be the source of the money that goes into the trust. The
resource hub explains those tools separately.
