## What a will is

A will is a written document that says what should happen to the things you own
after you die. It names the people who will carry out your wishes, and it sets
out how your money and belongings are shared.

Without a will, the law decides how things are shared. That may not match what
you would have wanted, and it can take longer to sort out.

## When it tends to matter

A will tends to matter when you want a say in any of the following:

- Who looks after your affairs after you die.
- How your money and belongings are shared.
- Who is named as a guardian for a child under 21 (see the testamentary
  guardianship topic).
- How you provide for a family member who may need ongoing support, including
  the person you care for.

## Thinking about the person you care for

If the person you care for may need long-term support, a will is often where
people start to think about how that support continues. Wherever it is
possible, it can help to include the person being cared for in the
conversation, in a way that suits them. Their views matter, and planning works
best when it reflects what they want for their own life.
