## What testamentary guardianship is

Testamentary guardianship is a way for a parent to name, in their will, a
person who would act as guardian for their child if the parent dies while the
child is under 21. The word testamentary simply means it is done through a
will.

Naming a guardian is a way of saying, in advance, who you would want to step in
and care for a child, rather than leaving that question open.

## When it tends to matter

This tends to matter when:

- A child is under 21.
- A parent wants a say in who would care for the child if the parent could no
  longer do so.
- A family wants to reduce uncertainty for the child during a difficult time.

## Thinking about the person being cared for

For a child with Autism Spectrum Disorder, continuity of care and familiar
routines can matter a great deal. Naming a guardian who understands the child's
needs is one way families try to protect that continuity. As the child grows,
and as they approach adulthood, it can help to think about how their own voice
and choices are included in planning, in a way that suits them.

When a young person reaches adulthood, different tools, such as an LPA or
deputyship, may become more relevant. See those topics.
