## What a trust is

A trust is an arrangement where one or more people, called trustees, look after
money or property on behalf of someone else, called the beneficiary. The
trustees hold and manage what is in the trust, and they use it for the
beneficiary in the way the trust sets out.

You can think of it as setting something aside, with clear instructions, and
asking trusted people to manage it carefully over time.

## When it tends to matter

A trust often comes up when someone wants to:

- Provide for a family member over a long period, rather than all at once.
- Make sure money is managed by people they trust, with care.
- Plan for the support of a person who may not manage a large sum on their own.

## Thinking about the person you care for

For a family planning around long-term care, a trust is one of the tools people
explore. The aim is usually steady, well-managed support that lasts. Where it
is possible, it can help to think about what kind of life the person being
cared for wants, and to involve them, so that the arrangement supports their
own goals and not only practical needs.

Trusts can be detailed, and the right structure depends on the family and the
amounts involved. This is an area where professional help is especially useful.
